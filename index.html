About

Who I Am

I am an Aerospace Engineering student at Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU), Ile-Ife, Nigeria, driven by a deep passion for understanding and shaping the technologies that define our future in the skies and beyond.

My interests span Satellite Technology, Aerospace Research, and Cybersecurity, fields I see as deeply interconnected in the era of connected systems and space infrastructure.

I believe in learning by building. From gliders to lunar rovers to nanosatellites, I bring ideas into the physical world and iterate until they fly, literally.

Research

Research Interests

ACTIVE — 2025–PRESENT

Space Debris Tracking & Autonomous Removal (LEO)

Researching vision-based tracking and autonomous rendezvous systems for non-cooperative tumbling space debris in Low Earth Orbit. The work focuses on GNC algorithms and proximity operations for safe debris capture — a growing challenge as orbital congestion increases.

ONGOING

Aircraft Flapping Wings Technology

Exploring biomimetic flight through the study of flapping wing mechanisms inspired by birds and insects. Research examines how oscillating wing designs can improve aerodynamic efficiency, maneuverability, and lift generation compared to conventional fixed and rotary wing systems.

ONGOING

Carbon Capture, Utilization and Storage (CCUS)

Exploring how carbon emissions can be captured, stored, and converted to alternative energy forms. Research aims to identify innovative pathways that bridge aerospace materials science with sustainable energy systems.
